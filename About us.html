History of Land Rover

Land Rover got its start as the Rover Company in 1885, which began as a bicycle manufacturer in Warwickshire, England. After changing ownership a few times over decades of history, Land Rover is now owned by the Indian auto manufacturing giant Tata Motors. Find out more about Land Rover history and origins below, and contact Land Rover Mission Viejo near Coto De Caza for all your Land Rover needs!

Who is TATA Motors

Tata Motors, which is owned by Tata Group, is an Indian automotive manufacturer headquartered in Mumbai that began all the way back in 1868 as a trading company. Below are some facts about the company that owns Land Rover:
-->Tata Group made a name for themselves by opening India’s first hotel with electricity in 1907, building Western India’s first hydropower plant, and founding the Indian Institute of Science in 1911.
-->Land Rover, along with Jaguar Cars, was bought by Tata Motors from Ford in 2008.
-->The two British brands were joined under Tata Motors to become Jaguar Land Rover Limited in 2013.
-->Tata Motors also owns multiple global automotive operations in both the commercial and passenger vehicle segments, while the parent Tata Group does business in everything from hospitals to steel to telecommunications.

Where is Land Rover Made

Land Rover manufactures new vehicles primarily in England, but their growing popularity over the past two or three decades has expanded their manufacturing to four other countries outside the UK.
-->United Kingdom, Solihull Plant — Range Rover, Range Rover Sport, and Range Rover Velar.
-->United Kingdom, Halewood Plant — Land Rover Discovery Sport and Range Rover Evoque.
-->Slovakia, Nitra Plant — Land Rover Discovery and Land Rover Defender.
-->Brazil, Itatiatia Plant — Land Rover Discovery Sport and Range Rover Evoque.
-->China, Changshu Plant — Land Rover Discovery Sport and Range Rover Evoque.
-->India, Pune Plant — Land Rover Discovery Sport and Range Rover Evoque.
